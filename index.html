A field guide to

OLOD & the Digital Accessibility Project

The Office of Organizational Learning and Development (OLOD) is part of the Office of the Associate Dean for Extension. OLOD works with the College of Agricultural Sciences (CAES), Family and Consumer Sciences (FACS), and other units to support Cooperative Extension across UGA — helping with program, staff, and leadership development through evidence-based practices and evaluation.

Stop 1

What OLOD does and why it exists

  • UGA Cooperative Extension was created in 1914 to serve Georgia's families, farmers, and businesses by sharing the latest information from its programs.
  • Extension covers three main areas: 4-H Youth Development, Agricultural & Natural Resources, and Family & Consumer Sciences.
  • UGA's faculty and staff with extension appointments have reached homes, families, farms, and businesses through research and education on food safety, disease and disaster prevention, community building, and well-being.
  • They work with people all across the state of Georgia, and their roles require constant contact with the public. Because of that, everything they produce needs to be accessible to everyone — no matter their ability, the senses they rely on, or how they consume and interact with it.
  • OLOD staff and faculty help Extension build communication skills, develop leadership, and evaluate programs.
  • This summer, OLOD partnered with the CAES Office of Information Technology (OIT) on an exploratory project on digital accessibility. The goal was to understand Extension staff and faculty's current practices around digital accessibility, and how to better support new training tailored to their reality.

Stop 4

Why does OLOD's work matter?

Apr 26, 2027 Law will take effect

The federal law, ADA Title II, requires that all public digital content be accessible. It took effect on April 26, 2026.

This means that websites, documents, videos, and social media posts created by UGA Extension must be accessible to people with disabilities.

Because Extension reaches people across all of Georgia, making content accessible means everyone can use it, no matter their ability.

This project's goal was to develop and support Extension faculty and staff's capacity to create accessible content, so their work reaches everyone it's meant to help witout barriers.
